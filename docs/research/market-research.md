# Survey + Market Research Pack

Date: 2026-04-11

## Goal

Collect lightweight evidence for:

- feasibility
- practicality
- user trust
- market demand

This document is for field research and handoff, not the final report.

## Target Respondents

Primary group:

- students who drive regularly
- commuters
- parents who drive family members

Optional secondary group:

- taxi drivers
- delivery drivers

Recommended sample size:

- survey: `30-50` respondents
- short interviews: `5-10` respondents

## Survey Setup

Recommended tool:

- Google Forms or Microsoft Forms

Keep the survey under 3 minutes.
Do not ask for identifying personal information unless absolutely necessary.

## Suggested Survey Questions

1. How often do you drive each week?
2. What type of driving do you do most often?
3. Have you ever experienced a crash or serious near-miss?
4. Would you install a phone app that detects a severe collision and starts a help countdown?
5. Would you allow continuous motion sensing while driving?
6. Would you allow location access only during an emergency event?
7. Which concern matters most to you?
8. Which failure would bother you more: false alarms or missed crashes?
9. Would you prefer alerting emergency contacts, emergency services, or both?
10. Would you pay for this feature, or only use it if free?

Recommended answer choices:

- weekly driving frequency:
  - daily
  - 3-5 times per week
  - 1-2 times per week
  - rarely
- main concern:
  - privacy
  - battery drain
  - false alarms
  - missing real crashes
  - complexity
- payment preference:
  - free only
  - one-time low-cost purchase
  - monthly subscription
  - bundled with another safety app

## Short Interview Prompts

Use these after the survey with a few participants:

- When would you trust this app and when would you not trust it?
- Would you keep your phone mounted while driving?
- What would make you uninstall this app quickly?
- Would you want automatic emergency contact alerts or a confirmation countdown first?
- In your view, what is worse: the app missing a real event or causing false alarms?

## What To Measure

At minimum, summarize:

- willingness to install
- willingness to grant motion permissions
- willingness to grant location permissions
- strongest adoption concern
- preference for emergency-contact workflow
- willingness to pay

## Competitor Matrix Template

Build a one-page comparison with these products:

- Apple Crash Detection

Suggested columns:

- product
- supported devices
- automatic detection
- emergency-contact workflow
- emergency-service workflow
- location sharing
- privacy posture
- pricing
- notable limitation
